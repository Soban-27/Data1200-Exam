Disadvantages of implementing AI in health care


Artificial intelligence (AI) is rapidly becoming a good thing for society. With the artificial intelligence in health care, it can help the doctors receive information on the patients easier and faster instead of reading through dozens of documents before a patient visit. It can decrease the risk of human failure on the receiving information or decision on what type of medical program the patient should have.



AI is already helping the doctors on receiving information on the patient, compressing the information and pulling out the important information that the doctors should have. But this can come with the risk of information leak on the patients. As the AI also has faults and risks for implanting this technology.



We know that artificial intelligence doesn't always have the best solutions for the patients even with all the information on the patients. The AI can also suggest the wrong drug for the patient or even the wrong medical program, which can lead to patient injury or declining health later on. It can also happen that AI can fail to notice a tumor on a radiological scan which can lead to a big error on the machine. It may also be a problem for all other patients if one AI system comes up with an error which can injure more patients than a single human error.


Privacy and Security Concerns:

AI systems in healthcare often involve the processing and analysis of sensitive patient data. Ensuring the privacy and security of this data is a significant concern. Any breaches or unauthorized access to patient information could lead to serious consequences, including identity theft or exposure of confidential medical histories.

Privacy Issues

Lack of Human Touch and Empathy:

AI systems may lack the human touch and empathy that is crucial in healthcare interactions. While AI can analyze data and provide insights, it may struggle to understand and respond to the emotional and psychological aspects of patient care. This could potentially impact the doctor-patient relationship and the overall quality of patient care.

Lack of Decision Making

Bias and Fairness Issues:

AI systems can inadvertently perpetuate or even exacerbate existing biases in healthcare. If the training data used to develop AI algorithms is biased, the AI system may produce biased results. This could lead to disparities in diagnosis and treatment, affecting certain demographic groups more than others. Ensuring fairness and mitigating bias in AI algorithms is a complex challenge.

Bias and fairness issues

Dependency and Job Displacement:

The widespread adoption of AI in healthcare may lead to concerns about job displacement for certain roles. Tasks traditionally performed by humans, such as data analysis, may be automated by AI systems. This could result in a shift in job roles, requiring healthcare professionals to adapt to new technologies. It is essential to consider the potential impact on the workforce and implement strategies to reskill and upskill workers.
